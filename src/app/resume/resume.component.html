  • I spent several weeks fighting with CSS, but now it's my favorite language
  • I remember how excited I was to see any type of code display on the screen!
  • I made a few class projects here, but none of them are presentable
    • (one of them was literally about cat costumes)
  • The only reason I enrolled in any sort of coding program was to enrich my graphic design resume, but then I fell in love with coding. It's one of the only things in my life that drives me to be better and truly problem solve
  • I graduated CoderGirl in the summer of 2018 and returned to my graphic design job that I had at the time
    • During this time, I would help when needed on JavaScript projects by providing feedback on the office's website and worked to get better UX with the rest of the web team

  • Although CSS became my favorite language during this time, it was a great accomplishment just to see something on the internet

2019: The Year of Full Time Web Development

  • I started working for SAIC in January 2019 and was immediately thrown into every part of the development lifecycle
    • Our team consists of two developers, so we were taking on many tasks all at once of various levels of the lifecycle
  • Our web team inherited an HTML/CSS/Javascript website and we decided to rewrite the entire thing using Angular 8
  • From learning how to install Node.js and Angular CLI to using Pivitol Cloud Foundry to push our site live, we learned everything as we went
    • This means that I have intimate knowledge of every part of Angular since I have had to cherry pick things like versions of CLI, scripts, and styles
    • Due to this, I have intermediate experience with Node.js and how it installs and updates
  • Working with Angular greatly improved my JavaScript skills and taught me how to work with property binding
  • I conducted several meetings with my customers to show them how SPAs are a better way to provide information to our users
  • All this new knowledge led to some questionable styling choices, as demonstrated in this div. I was mostly just excited to have learned an entirely new framework. It seemed digestable styles were secondary...

2020: The Year of Angular...

  • We launched our new Angular 8 site in January of 2020 to great reviews
  • Prior to this, we conducted months of user testing by having biweekly web meetings in which we showed progress of our site to our customers for their approval
    • We had a unique user testing experience in that we were developing, conducting UX testing, and collecting content
    • I feel the fact that I was able to work on a two-person team and have this kind of workflow speaks to my commitment to all aspects of web development
  • Being a graphic designer, I created web graphics for this site and worked to incorporate visual elements to include Material icons
  • Aimed to make our site visually interesting and easy to digest for the user by making only one component visible at a time where applicable
  • As a small web team, we were responsible for three different websites with three different categories of content
    • We had an externally facing website for the public, one for the internal workforce, and another classified website specifically for classified content
    • We worked with GeoAxis and other authentication services to provide the utmost in security access
